Polaris

Structured crop-supply monitoring and redistribution planning for local government units.

Overview

Polaris tracks crop supply against ideal targets for each LGU. Positive balances identify oversupply, negative balances identify shortages, and the resulting priorities help surface transfer opportunities between areas.

Key Capabilities

  • Adds or updates per-LGU crop supply records.
  • Lists current balances and identifies the most critical shortage.
  • Matches crop oversupply with shortage locations.
  • Persists records through a SQLite repository.
  • Supports both command-line and programmatic service usage.

Technical Approach

The Python package separates domain rules, application orchestration, CLI entrypoints, and persistence. Priority-based indexing keeps shortage and oversupply decisions explicit, while compatibility facades preserve previous imports as the project moves toward a cleaner layered architecture.

Stack

Python, SQLite, command-line interfaces, priority queues, layered architecture, and pytest.
