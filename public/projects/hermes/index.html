Project HERMES

Hazard and Emergency Reporting, Monitoring, and Evaluation System. A real-time disaster communication control center that turns incoming reports into actionable incident intelligence.

Overview

HERMES was built to help responders and coordinators manage emergency communication with less delay and less ambiguity. Reports from chat and user submissions are organized into structured incidents, making it easier to monitor situations, escalate issues, and keep teams aligned during fast-moving events.

What Was Built

  • Role-based access control for responders, operators, and administrators.
  • Real-time incident reporting and monitoring flows backed by Supabase.
  • Structured entities for hazards, reports, evaluation states, and response updates.
  • Backend logic designed to support operational coordination rather than just raw message storage.

Outcome

The project clarified how disaster-domain language should map into system objects and permissions, which made the platform easier to reason about and safer to extend as new workflows were added.
